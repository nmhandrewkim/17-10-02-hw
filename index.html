Stromboli

Ingredients:

  • Fail-Proof Pizza Dough:

    • 1 cup water, room temperature
    • 9/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
    • 1 tablespoon honey or sugar
    • 2 teaspoons salt
    • 2 tablespoons olive or vegetable oil
    • 3 cups all-purpose or bread flour
  • Pepperoni
  • Salami
  • Prosciutto
  • 3/2 cups shredded mozzarella
  • Spicy pepper jack cheese
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons pizza sauce
  • Crushed red pepper (optional)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • All-purpose flour

Directions:

  1. View directions for bread dough, courtesy of Lauren's Latest
  2. Preheat oven to 500 degrees
  3. Divide dough in half and mold into a hemisphere
  4. Flour a large sheet of parchment paper to prevent dough from sticking
  5. Place dough on parchment paper and sprinkle flour on the dough
  6. Using a rolling pin, roll dough from the center outwards until dough is rectangular and less than half an inch thick
  7. Using a spoon, spread pizza sauce onto the dough. Leave a one and a half inch to two inch border clear of any sauce or ingredients. Do not use too much sauce; excessive use of sauce will result in a soggy pastry!
  8. Spread mozzarella on top of the sauce
  9. Place lines of pepperoni, salami, and prosciutto across the mozzarella. Make sure to cover the mozzarella.
  10. Spread slices of pepper jack on top of the protein
  11. Use a brush to brush the beaten egg on the dough, around the cheese
  12. Fold the edge of the dough that's furthest away from you on itself to half the length of the border
  13. Brush the folded edge with the beaten egg
  14. Gripping the side of the parchment paper that's furthest away from you, lift the paper to start folding the stromboli
  15. Roll the stromboli towards yourself. Make sure that no ingredients spill
  16. Before rolling the stromboli for the last time, fold in the horizontal edges and brush with the beaten egg
  17. Finish rolling the stromboli
  18. Place the parchment paper with the stromboli on a baking sheet.
  19. Make sure the seam of the stromboli is facing down
  20. Brush the top and sides of the stromboli with the beaten egg
  21. Score the top of the stromboli with diagonal cuts no more than half an inch deep
  22. Sprinkle parsley and remainder of mozzarella on top of the stromboli
  23. Bake for 13-15 minutes or until crust is golden brown
  24. Remove from oven and let cool for ten minutes before serving

 The stromboli is a pastry made by placing fillings on a piece of dough, folding the dough over, sealing, and baking it. Fillings typically include various cheeses - such as mozzarella - and Italian meats such as prosciutto, pepperoni, salami, or pancetta. The dish was invented in the United States during the 1950s, and was named after the Italian film Stromboli.
